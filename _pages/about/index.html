All about writing story

By John Powell |18 October 2015

We are a group of people from all kinds of backgrounds who are committed to living out the Christian faith in a relevant and practical way.

Our vision is…

… to be filled with God’s presence and power, through which lives and communities will be transformed.

As a family, we want people to be encouraged, through discipleship, to be authentic followers of Jesus, so that they can play their full part in God’s plan.

Our values are…

  1. Identity: Belper Community Church is a place where everyone knows that they are sons and daughters of God, and that they belong and have a role to play.
  2. Discipleship: Belper Community Church is a place where people will grow up into maturity and develop in their knowledge and love of Christ and each other
  3. Family: Belper Community Church is a safe place where everyone knows they belong.
  4. Mission: For members of BCC to be a positive witness to Christ in their day-to-day lives. We expect this to be accompanied by demonstrations of God’s power both in church and outside.
  5. Presence: Belper Community Church should be Spirit-filled; with our lives and our worship impacted on a daily basis by the Holy Spirit working supernaturally.
  6. Transformation: Belper Community Church will be involved with and integrated into our community – being a faithful presence and making a positive impact on the people and the environment.

We work in many practical ways along side other churches in Belper. We are also part of East Midlands Christian Fellowships on a regional level, and Salt and Light Ministries on an international level.

We want to reach out to others and share the love of God with them, both through what we are saying and what we do. We are a truly intergenerational family from newborns to grannies and grandads! We look to be more than a Sunday morning church; getting together regularly in weekly smaller groups.

Belper Community Church started around a couple who became Christians on Alpha. A few years later, along with friendships, new babies and God-inspired connections and moves, the family is growing rapidly so that we have moved to meet at the Whitemoor Centre.
